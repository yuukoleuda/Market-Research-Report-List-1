<p><p>Gene therapy for ovarian cancer involves the delivery of genetic material to target cancer cells, utilizing various administration routes. Intravenous gene therapy delivers therapeutic genes through the bloodstream, allowing systemic distribution. Intratumoral therapy directly injects genetic agents into the tumor, enhancing localized treatment effectiveness. Intraperitoneal administration involves delivering gene therapy into the abdominal cavity, targeting ovarian cancer that often metastasizes there. Each method aims to improve treatment outcomes by specifically targeting cancerous cells while minimizing systemic side effects.</p></p>
