<p><p>The solid surface market can be categorized into two primary types: casting molding and extrusion molding. Casting molding involves pouring liquid acrylic or polyester resins into molds, allowing for intricate shapes and designs, which are often used in countertops and sinks. Conversely, extrusion molding involves forcing a solid surface material through a die, producing continuous sheets or shapes with uniform thickness. Each method offers distinct advantages in terms of design flexibility and production efficiency, catering to diverse applications in construction and interior design.</p></p>
