<p><p>Optical tweezers and magnetic tweezers are essential tools in mechanobiology, used to manipulate biological samples at the nanoscale. Optical tweezers utilize focused laser beams to trap and move particles, enabling precise manipulation of cells and biomolecules. Magnetic tweezers, on the other hand, employ magnetic fields to control magnetic beads attached to biological samples, allowing the study of molecular interactions and mechanical properties. Together, they facilitate advanced research in cell mechanics, molecular biology, and biophysics, enhancing our understanding of biological processes.</p></p>
