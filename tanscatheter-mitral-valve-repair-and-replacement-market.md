<p><p>The Transcatheter Mitral Valve Repair and Replacement market encompasses two primary valve types: mechanical valves and bioprosthetic tissue valves. Mechanical valves are durable devices made from synthetic materials, designed for long-term use and often requiring lifelong anticoagulation therapy. In contrast, bioprosthetic tissue valves, derived from animal tissues or human donors, offer a more natural function and generally do not require extensive anticoagulation, appealing to patients with specific health concerns. Both options address mitral valve dysfunction through less invasive procedures.</p></p>
